GE Lights for Life challenge 2016

The Lights for Life challenge, organized by GE, challenges participants to rethink the future of lights. Our idea has to be an adaptation of C technology by GE.

After weeks of brainstorming, our team conveiced Lumiere, a smart light that administers phototherapy to patients in hospitals and nursing homes. Lighting plays an important role in influencing the mood and sleep patterns of patients. This in turn would affect patient's quality of stay and rate of recovery.

At the heart of Lumiere lies the gateway. It is a small unit at the patient's bedside which boosts wifi capabilities and an accelerometer. The gateway facilitates communication between Lumiere and the cloud, allowing a robust communication protocols. In version 1, we feature a sleep pattern tracker which analyzes patient's sleep cycles through vibrations and control lighting conditions to suitable levels.

I designed the entire package for Lumiere. I used SOLIDWORKS, a CAD software, to illustrate Lumiere, the gateway as well as the hospital set-up, as seen on the images. I also contributed to the ideation process and market research.
